The Role of Geometry in Abstract Art

Abstract art often relies on geometric principles to organize space and guide the viewer's eye. Artists such as Piet Mondrian and Kazimir Malevich reduced forms to essential shapes, believing that geometry could reveal universal truths. The grid, in particular, became a recurring motif, representing order, balance, and rationality within abstraction.

These geometric systems were not merely aesthetic choices; they reflected philosophical and even spiritual ideals. For many modernist artists, reducing the visual language to squares, circles, and triangles was a way of seeking purity in form.
