Origins of Abstract Expressionism

Emerging in 1940s New York, Abstract Expressionism marked a shift in artistic power from Europe to America. Influenced by surrealism, this movement emphasized subconscious creation and emotional intensity through bold brushwork and expansive canvases.

Key Figures in the Movement

Jackson Pollock's drip paintings, Mark Rothko’s color fields, and Willem de Kooning’s aggressive brushstrokes helped define the movement. Each artist brought a unique style, but shared a desire to break from traditional representation.

Lasting Legacy

Abstract Expressionism paved the way for future contemporary art forms such as Minimalism and Conceptual Art. Its emphasis on process, gesture, and emotional resonance remains a cornerstone of modern art education and practice.
