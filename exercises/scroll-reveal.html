History of Art Movements

Scroll down to discover five major transformations in art history.

Renaissance

Marking a rebirth of classical knowledge and humanism, Renaissance art emphasized proportion, perspective, and anatomical precision. Artists like Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo redefined realism in painting and sculpture.

Baroque

Emerging in 17th-century Europe, Baroque art was characterized by dramatic lighting, emotional intensity, and grandeur. Caravaggio, Rembrandt, and Bernini created works that engaged viewers physically and emotionally.

Impressionism

Challenging academic traditions, Impressionist painters like Monet and Degas captured fleeting moments using light, loose brushwork, and outdoor settings. The movement emphasized perception over perfection.

Surrealism

Influenced by psychoanalysis and dreams, Surrealist artists like Dalí and Magritte explored the subconscious mind through bizarre, illogical imagery that blurred the boundaries between reality and imagination.

Contemporary Art

Contemporary art defies clear definitions, encompassing conceptual, digital, and interdisciplinary practices. It often engages with social, political, and environmental issues, pushing the limits of medium and message.
