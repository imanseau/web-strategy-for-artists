The Art of Raku Firing

Raku is a traditional Japanese pottery technique that emphasizes spontaneity and natural aesthetics. Unlike conventional kiln firings, Raku pieces are removed from the kiln while still glowing hot and subjected to post-firing reductions, creating unique patterns of crackle and glaze.

This process, rooted in Zen philosophy, embraces imperfection and the unpredictable nature of fire. Artists who practice Raku are not merely crafting ceramics — they are collaborating with the elements to produce one-of-a-kind works that reflect both intention and chance.
